Rome & Amsterdam Trip

  • Written May 10, 2015
  • Comments
  • travel

Rome, and Italy in general, had been on our shortlist of places we were aching for a chance to visit. For Marilyn’s yearly work trip to Amsterdam, we decided to tack on an extra week and visit the city and see the sites. The ancient sites of Rome have always had a draw to them. The Colosseum, Pompeii, the Vatican, the Parthenon — not to mention exploring the city in general had us excited from when we first started planning out the trip.

The Trip

For this vacation, we didn’t jump around quite as much as we did for our Japan trip last year. We spent a week at an AirBnb a block away from Barberini station — close to the Spanish Steps and Villa Borghese. We spent the days treking out from here, but mostly staying in the city. We did take a day trip out to Pompeii, which was also amazing.

Later on, we made our way up to Amsterdam via a quick direct flight. Marilyn was in a corporate hotel for her time there, and I found a nearby AirBnb in the attic of an old building. I spent the days exploring the city while Marilyn worked, hitting up everywhere I could despite the cold January weather.
